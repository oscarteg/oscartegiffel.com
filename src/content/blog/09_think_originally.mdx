I watched a video by Odysseas that got me thinking about how original ideas actually form. Five habits stood out.

First, __be selective about what you feed your mind__. If you read the same things as everyone else, you'll think the same things. Go sideways -- unusual topics, overlooked genres, things outside your field. The more distinct your inputs, the more distinct your output.

Second, __create space for thinking__. It's easy to fill every gap with consumption -- podcasts on the commute, videos before bed, articles at lunch. The processing happens in the gaps, not the intake. You need quiet time for ideas to actually form.

Third -- __get out and experience things__. Sitting at a desk looking for inspiration is the wrong setup. Walking somewhere new, talking to someone with a completely different life -- that's where the material comes from.

Fourth, __start before you're ready__. The act of making something generates more ideas than thinking about making it ever does. You can't see the next step until you take the first one.

Finally, __lean into what makes you different__. The strange overlap between two things you care about that nobody usually connects -- that's where original ideas live.
